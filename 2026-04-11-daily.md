### 1. SkillClaw: Let Skills Evolve Collectively with Agentic Evolver
- **Authors:** Ziyu Ma, Shidong Yang, Yuxiang Ji, Xucong Wang, Yong Wang, Yiming Hu, Tongwen Huang, Xiangxiang Chu
- **Upvotes:** 194 (Daily Rank #1)
- **URL:** https://huggingface.co/papers/2604.08377
- **Abstract:** Large language model (LLM) agents such as OpenClaw rely on reusable skills to perform complex tasks, yet these skills remain largely static after deployment. SkillClaw presents a framework for collective skill evolution in multi-user agent ecosystems, which treats cross-user and over-time interactions as the primary signal for improving skills. SkillClaw continuously aggregates trajectories generated during use and processes them with an autonomous evolver, which identifies recurring behavioral patterns and translates them into updates to the skill set. The resulting skills are maintained in a shared repository and synchronized across users, enabling cross-user knowledge transfer and cumulative capability improvement. Experiments on WildClawBench show significant performance improvements for Qwen3-Max in real-world agent scenarios.
- **Keywords:** large language model agents, reusable skills, multi-user agent ecosystems, autonomous evolver, cross-user knowledge transfer

### 2. Rethinking Generalization in Reasoning SFT: A Conditional Analysis on Optimization, Data, and Model Capability
- **Authors:** Qihan Ren, Peng Wang, Ruikun Cai, Shuai Shao, Dadi Guo, Yuejin Xie, Yafu Li, Quanshi Zhang, Xia Hu, Jing Shao, Dongrui Liu
- **Upvotes:** 168 (Daily Rank #2)
- **URL:** https://huggingface.co/papers/2604.06628
- **Abstract:** A prevailing narrative in LLM post-training holds that supervised finetuning (SFT) memorizes while reinforcement learning (RL) generalizes. This paper revisits this claim for reasoning SFT with long chain-of-thought (CoT) supervision and finds that cross-domain generalization is not absent but conditional, jointly shaped by optimization dynamics, training data, and base-model capability. Key findings include: (1) a dip-and-recovery pattern where cross-domain performance first degrades before recovering with extended training; (2) data quality matters significantly—low-quality solutions hurt generalization while verified long-CoT traces yield consistent gains; (3) model capability is essential—stronger models internalize transferable procedural patterns. Notably, generalization is asymmetric: reasoning improves while safety degrades.
- **Keywords:** supervised finetuning, reinforcement learning, cross-domain generalization, long chain-of-thought, optimization dynamics

### 3. HY-Embodied-0.5: Embodied Foundation Models for Real-World Agents
- **Authors:** Tencent Hunyuan Team
- **URL:** https://huggingface.co/papers/2604.07430
- **Abstract:** Introduction of HY-Embodied-0.5, a family of foundation models specifically designed for real-world embodied agents. The suite comprises two variants: an efficient 2B activated parameter model for edge deployment, and a powerful 32B activated parameter model for complex reasoning. Key innovations include: (1) Mixture-of-Transformers (MoT) architecture for modality-specific computing; (2) visual latent tokens for enhanced perceptual representation; (3) iterative, self-evolving post-training paradigm; (4) on-policy distillation from large to small model. Extensive evaluations across 22 benchmarks show the MoT-2B model outperforms similarly sized SOTA models on 16 benchmarks, while the 32B variant achieves performance comparable to Gemini 3.0 Pro. Downstream robot control experiments demonstrate compelling results in real-world physical evaluations.
- **Keywords:** embodied AI, vision-language models, robotics, Mixture-of-Transformers, edge deployment
