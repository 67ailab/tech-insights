### 1. ClawBench: Can AI Agents Complete Everyday Online Tasks?
- **Authors:** Yuxuan Zhang, Yubo Wang, Yipeng Zhu, Penghui Du, Junwen Miao, Xuan Lu, Wendong Xu, Yunzhuo Hao, Songcheng Cai, Xiaochen Wang, Huaisong Zhang, Xian Wu, Yi Lu, Minyi Lei, Kai Zou, Huifeng Yin, Ping Nie, Liang Chen, Dongfu Jiang, Wenhu Chen, Kelsey R. Allen
- **URL:** https://huggingface.co/papers/2604.08523
- **Abstract:** AI agents may automate your inbox, but can they automate other routine aspects of your life? ClawBench introduces an evaluation framework of 153 simple tasks people accomplish regularly, spanning 144 live platforms across 15 categories—from purchases and appointments to job applications. Unlike existing benchmarks using offline sandboxes with static pages, ClawBench operates on production websites, preserving real-world complexity, dynamics, and challenges. A lightweight interception layer captures and blocks only final submission requests, ensuring safe evaluation without real-world side effects. Evaluations of 7 frontier models show both proprietary and open-source models complete only a small portion of tasks: Claude Sonnet 4.6 achieves 33.3%, while GPT-5.4 scores just 6.5%. Progress on ClawBench brings us closer to AI agents functioning as reliable general-purpose assistants.
- **Keywords:** AI agents, web automation, benchmark, real-world evaluation, task completion

### 2. SkillClaw: Let Skills Evolve Collectively with Agentic Evolver
- **Authors:** Ziyu Ma, Shidong Yang, Yuxiang Ji, Xucong Wang, Yong Wang, Yiming Hu, Tongwen Huang, Xiangxiang Chu
- **URL:** https://huggingface.co/papers/2604.08377
- **Abstract:** Large language model agents such as OpenClaw rely on reusable skills to perform complex tasks, yet these skills remain largely static after deployment. SkillClaw presents a framework for collective skill evolution in multi-user agent ecosystems, treating cross-user and over-time interactions as the primary signal for improving skills. SkillClaw continuously aggregates trajectories generated during use and processes them with an autonomous evolver, which identifies recurring behavioral patterns and translates them into updates to the skill set. Resulting skills are maintained in a shared repository and synchronized across users, enabling cross-user knowledge transfer and cumulative capability improvement. Experiments on WildClawBench show significant performance improvements for Qwen3-Max in real-world agent scenarios.
- **Keywords:** large language model agents, reusable skills, multi-user agent ecosystems, autonomous evolver, cross-user knowledge transfer

- **URL:** https://huggingface.co/papers/2604.07430
- **Abstract:** Introduction of HY-Embodied-0.5, a family of foundation models specifically designed for real-world embodied agents.
