### 1. VEGA-3D: Generation Models Know Space
- **Authors:** Dingkang Liang et al. (Huazhong University of Science and Technology, Baidu Inc.)
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.19235
- **Abstract:** VEGA-3D proposes leveraging implicit spatial priors within large-scale video generation models for 3D scene understanding. The framework repurposes pre-trained video diffusion models as "Latent World Simulators" to enrich Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) with dense geometric cues without explicit 3D supervision. Key innovation: token-level adaptive gated fusion mechanism integrates spatiotemporal features with semantic representations. Results show superior performance on 3D scene understanding, spatial reasoning, and embodied manipulation benchmarks—critical for agentic systems operating in physical environments.

### 2. SAMA: Factorized Semantic Anchoring and Motion Alignment for Instruction-Guided Video Editing
- **Authors:** Xinyao Zhang et al. (Baidu, Tsinghua University, City University of Hong Kong, Zhejiang University)
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.19228
- **Abstract:** SAMA introduces a factorized framework for instruction-guided video editing that separates semantic planning from motion modeling. The approach uses Semantic Anchoring (predicting semantic tokens at sparse anchor frames) and Motion Alignment (motion-centric video restoration pre-training) to achieve state-of-the-art performance among open-source models, competitive with commercial systems like Kling-Omni. Notably, the factorized pre-training alone yields strong zero-shot video editing capabilities, suggesting robust instruction-following can emerge from learning disentangled semantic and motion representations—relevant for agentic video manipulation tasks.

### 3. FASTER: Rethinking Real-Time Flow VLAs
- **Authors:** Zhe Liu et al. (The University of Hong Kong, ACE Robotics)
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.19199
- **Abstract:** FASTER addresses the critical latency bottleneck in Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models for real-time robotic deployment. The method introduces a Horizon-Aware Schedule that adaptively prioritizes near-term actions during flow sampling, compressing Time to First Action (TTFA) to single-step sampling while preserving long-horizon trajectory quality. Real-world experiments on table tennis tasks demonstrate 10× acceleration compared to baseline π0.5 and X-VLA models. This work is highly relevant for embodied AI agents requiring rapid reaction to dynamic environments, enabling deployment on consumer-grade GPUs.
