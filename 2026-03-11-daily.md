### 2. Omni-Diffusion: Unified Multimodal Understanding and Generation with Masked Discrete Diffusion
- **Authors:** Lijiang Li, Zuwei Long, Yunhang Shen, Heting Gao, Haoyu Cao, Xing Sun, Caifeng Shan, Ran He, Chaoyou Fu
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.06577
- **Upvotes:** 35
- **Abstract:** Omni-Diffusion introduces the first any-to-any multimodal language model built entirely on mask-based discrete diffusion models, unifying understanding and generation across text, speech, and images. The model employs a unified mask-based discrete diffusion approach to directly capture the joint distribution over discrete multimodal tokens, supporting both bimodal and complex multi-modality scenarios. On diverse benchmarks, the method outperforms or matches existing multimodal systems, highlighting the promise of diffusion models for next-generation multimodal foundation models.
- **Keywords:** multimodal large language models, discrete diffusion models, mask-based diffusion, multimodal foundation models

### 3. Thinking to Recall: How Reasoning Unlocks Parametric Knowledge in LLMs
- **Authors:** Zorik Gekhman, Roee Aharoni, Eran Ofek, Mor Geva, Roi Reichart, Jonathan Herzig (Google)
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.09906
- **Upvotes:** 28
- **Abstract:** This paper studies how reasoning expands LLMs' parametric recall boundary on simple factual questions that don't require step-by-step solutions. The research identifies two key mechanisms: (1) computational buffer effect, where models use generated reasoning tokens for latent computation independent of semantic content; and (2) factual priming, where generating topically related facts acts as a semantic bridge for correct answer retrieval. Importantly, hallucinating intermediate facts during reasoning increases final answer hallucinations, but accuracy can be improved by prioritizing reasoning trajectories with hallucination-free factual statements.
- **Keywords:** large language models, parametric knowledge recall, reasoning, computational buffer, factual priming, hallucination

### 4. MM-Zero: Self-Evolving Multi-Model Vision Language Models From Zero Data
- **Authors:** Zongxia Li, Hongyang Du, Chengsong Huang, Xiyang Wu, Lantao Yu, Yicheng He, Jing Xie, Xiaomin Wu, Zhichao Liu, Jiarui Zhang, Fuxiao Liu (NVIDIA)
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.09206
- **Upvotes:** 26
- **Abstract:** MM-Zero presents the first RL-based framework achieving zero-data self-evolution for Vision Language Model (VLM) reasoning. The multi-role self-evolving training framework comprises three specialized roles: a Proposer (generates visual concepts and questions), a Coder (translates concepts to executable code for rendering images), and a Solver (performs multimodal reasoning). All roles are initialized from the same base model and trained using Group Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO) with rewards integrating execution feedback, visual verification, and difficulty balancing. MM-Zero improves VLM reasoning across multimodal benchmarks, establishing a scalable path toward self-evolving multi-model systems.
- **Keywords:** self-evolving, vision language models, reinforcement learning, multimodal reasoning, GRPO, zero-data learning
