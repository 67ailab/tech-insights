## 🔥 Top Story: Karpathy Declares "Claws" a New Layer of the AI Stack

Andrej Karpathy posted a widely-discussed thread (922 comments on HN) about **"Claws"** — his term for the emerging category of OpenClaw-like personal agent systems that sit on top of LLM agents, adding orchestration, scheduling, context persistence, and tool calls.

> "Just like LLM agents were a new layer on top of LLMs, Claws are now a new layer on top of LLM agents, taking the orchestration, scheduling, context, tool calls and a kind of persistence to a next level."

Karpathy noted an explosion of smaller projects — NanoClaw (~4K lines of code), nanobot, zeroclaw, ironclaw, picoclaw — all vying to be the lightweight, auditable personal agent runtime. Simon Willison observed that "Claw" is becoming a term of art for AI agents running on personal hardware, communicating via messaging protocols, and capable of both direct instruction and scheduled tasks.

**Why it matters:** This signals a shift from cloud-hosted AI assistants to locally-run, always-on personal agent systems — a major architectural evolution.

## 📰 Hacker News Highlights

### A Development Methodology for the Agentic AI Era
A new post proposes a three-layer methodology for building with AI agents: **Output Layer** (human-defined contracts for correctness), **Input Layer** (strict schemas to prevent agent assumption drift), and the **Process Layer** (where agents do the work). The key insight: humans shift from "craftsmen" to "architects of correctness" — our primary artifact becomes the contract, not the code.

- [HN Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47115067)

### AI Agents vs. Agentic AI: A Conceptual Taxonomy (HuggingFace Papers)
Distinguishes between **AI Agents** (modular, task-specific, LLM-driven) and **Agentic AI** (multi-agent collaboration, dynamic task decomposition, persistent memory, orchestrated autonomy). Proposes solutions including ReAct loops, RAG, orchestration layers, and causal modeling for challenges like hallucination, brittleness, and coordination failure.
