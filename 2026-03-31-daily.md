## 📄 Top Hugging Face Papers (AI/Agent Research)

### 1. TAPS: Task Aware Proposal Distributions for Speculative Sampling
- **Upvotes:** 62 | **arXiv:** 2603.27027
- **Authors:** Mohamad Zbib, Mohamad Bazzi, Ammar Mohanna, Hasan Abed Al Kader Hammoud, Bernard Ghanem
- **URL:** https://huggingface.co/papers/2603.27027
- **Abstract:** Speculative decoding accelerates autoregressive generation by letting a lightweight draft model propose future tokens that a larger target model then verifies in parallel. This study shows that task-specific training yields clear specialization: MathInstruct-trained drafts excel on reasoning benchmarks, while ShareGPT-trained drafts perform best on MT-Bench. The key finding is that specialized drafters should be combined at inference time through confidence-based routing rather than weight-space averaging, achieving the highest acceptance lengths.
- **Significance:** Critical for improving AI agent response latency and throughput in production systems.

- **Abstract:** Introduces the first autonomous research framework tailored to clinical medicine. ### 3. Gen-Searcher: Reinforcing Agentic Search for Image Generation
- **Upvotes:** 39 | **arXiv:** 2603.28767
- **Authors:** Kaituo Feng, Manyuan Zhang, Shuang Chen, Yunlong Lin, Kaixuan Fan, Yilei Jiang, Hongyu Li, Dian Zheng, Chenyang Wang, Xiangyu Yue
- **URL:** https://huggingface.co/papers/2603.28767
- **Abstract:** Presents the first search-augmented image generation agent that performs multi-hop reasoning and search to collect textual knowledge and reference images for grounded generation. The model is trained with supervised fine-tuning followed by agentic reinforcement learning with dual reward feedback (text-based and image-based rewards). Gen-Searcher improves Qwen-Image by ~16 points on the KnowGen benchmark and 15 points on WISE.
- **Significance:** Demonstrates how agentic search can overcome the knowledge limitations of frozen image generation models, enabling more accurate and up-to-date visual content creation.
