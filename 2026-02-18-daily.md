### AI Agents vs. Agentic AI: A Conceptual Taxonomy
A structured taxonomy distinguishing **AI Agents** (modular, task-specific LLM-driven systems) from **Agentic AI** (multi-agent collaboration with dynamic task decomposition, persistent memory, and orchestrated autonomy). Covers applications from customer support to research automation and robotic coordination.

**Link:** https://huggingface.co/papers/2505.10468

### Agentic Refactoring: An Empirical Study
Large-scale study of 15,451 AI agent-generated refactorings across 12,256 pull requests in Java projects. Key findings:
- Agents explicitly target refactoring in **26.1% of commits**
- Dominated by low-level edits (variable renames, type changes) vs. human preference for high-level design changes
- Motivations: maintainability (52.5%), readability (28.1%)
- Small but statistically significant improvements in structural metrics

**Link:** https://huggingface.co/papers/2511.04824

### AstaBench: Rigorous Benchmarking of AI Agents
New benchmark suite with 2400+ problems spanning the entire scientific discovery process. Evaluation of 57 agents across 22 classes reveals that despite progress on individual aspects, **AI remains far from solving the challenge of science research assistance**.

**Link:** https://huggingface.co/papers/2510.21652

### AI Agents That Matter
Analysis of current agent benchmarks reveals: SOTA agents are needlessly complex and costly; narrow accuracy focus leads to mistaken conclusions; many benchmarks have inadequate holdout sets causing overfitting. Proposes jointly optimizing cost + accuracy.

**Link:** https://huggingface.co/papers/2407.01502
