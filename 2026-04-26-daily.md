### 3. Memory architecture is becoming a key differentiator
The Hugging Face papers point toward explicit memory systems, skill banks, and structured retrieval rather than purely larger base models. Personalized and long-lived agents will likely depend on better external memory design, not just wider context windows.

### 4. Safety infrastructure is expanding beyond model alignment
OpenAI Privacy Filter and the GPT-5.5 Bio Bug Bounty both reflect a broader shift toward deployment-layer safeguards: redaction, logging controls, red teaming, and specialized evaluation. The stack around the model is becoming just as important as the model itself.

### 5. AI is increasingly useful as a collaborator in expert work
The Erdős-problem story reinforces that AI’s value in research may come from proposing novel pathways, reframings, and heuristics that humans can validate. This is a strong pattern for agentic systems aimed at science, engineering, and analytical workflows.
