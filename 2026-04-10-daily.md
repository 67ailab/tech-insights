### 1. **Real-World Agent Performance Gap**

This suggests current agent evaluations are poor predictors of real-world utility. The gap between sandbox and production environments remains a major challenge.

### 2. **Embodied AI Maturation**
Tencent's HY-Embodied-0.5 demonstrates significant progress in embodied intelligence, with efficient 2B models suitable for edge deployment and 32B models matching frontier VLM performance. The focus on spatial/temporal perception and iterative self-evolving training reflects the field's shift toward practical, deployable embodied agents.

### 3. **Skill Evolution & Collective Intelligence**
SkillClaw represents an emerging paradigm: skills evolving through multi-agent collaboration rather than isolated training. This aligns with broader trends toward agentic systems that improve collectively, potentially accelerating capability development beyond single-agent learning limits.

### 4. **Protocol Standards Debate**
The HN discussion on MCP vs. skills reflects ongoing tension in the agent ecosystem: standardized protocols (MCP) promise interoperability and easier integration, while custom skills offer flexibility and optimization for specific use cases. This debate will shape agent architecture decisions in 2026.

### 5. **Reasoning SFT Nuances**
The SFT generalization research reveals that training dynamics are more complex than simple scaling. The dip-and-recovery pattern suggests checkpoint selection and data curation are critical—single-metric evaluations may miss important capability transitions during training.
